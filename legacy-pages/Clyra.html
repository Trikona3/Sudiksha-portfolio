Product overview

What is Clyra?

Clyra is an AI productivity assistant that turns incoming emails into structured, prioritized tasks and automatically slots them into your available calendar time. It blends email understanding with health and weather signals to build plans that are realistic and balanced, while an agentic UI proposes actions for you to approve—so you spend less time triaging and more time doing.

  • Email → task conversion with detailed labels
  • Health & weather–aware scheduling
  • Conflict-free placement across Google/Apple/Outlook calendars
  • Agent proposes & executes; you approve

Why it matters

Knowledge workers spend too much time translating emails into action. Clyra reduces cognitive load, automates prioritization, and keeps plans aligned with real-world context—resulting in fewer dropped balls, calmer days, and smoother handoffs.

Design Decisions

Email-to-action clarity first

Structured the core flow to convert unstructured email threads into clear tasks, labels, priorities, and timelines to reduce triage effort.

Human-in-control agent behavior

Designed agentic interactions around proposal + approval, so automation helps users move faster without removing user control.

Context-aware scheduling

Used health and weather context as inputs in planning so schedules feel realistic and sustainable, not just technically optimized.

Cross-calendar reliability

Prioritized conflict-free placement across Google, Apple, and Outlook calendars to support real-world adoption and reduce missed work.

User research

Study

AI Daily Assistant — User Needs (Maze)

n ≈ 14 respondents (per screenshot), 36 blocks.

Method

Quant + qual survey

Daily planning habits, email dependency, work–life balance, weather/health usage, pricing.

Key outcome

Strong intent to adopt

Email-driven tasks + calendar auto-scheduling rose to the top as must-haves.

Top insights

  • Email is a primary driver of work. Many respondents rely on email to know “what to do today,” yet feel their UI slows them down.
  • Manual triage is the biggest friction. People want long emails turned into tasks with due dates—without copy-pasting.
  • Scheduling support is valued. Interest in a tool that places tasks into available times automatically, with conflict resolution.
  • Well-being matters. Participants check weather frequently; a meaningful subset track health and believe it affects productivity.
  • Adoption & pricing. A freemium + premium model tested well; $10–$15/mo was commonly acceptable for automation + smart reminders.

Design implications

  • Prioritize email → task extraction accuracy and traceability.
  • Make auto-scheduling the default; keep a 1-tap manual override.
  • Surface energy/weather guardrails as gentle nudges, not blockers.
  • Offer a clear free tier with premium automation bundles.

Design decisions

Inbox triage first

Most days start in email. The primary action surfaces “Convert to tasks” with labels (owner, due, effort).

Calendar as source of truth

A single, editable plan. Reschedules preserve constraints (timezones, no-meeting windows, travel buffers).

Focus-forward UX

Big primary CTA per step, compact chip filters, and ambient progress to lower cognitive load.

Clear handoff

Redlines, component specs, and tokens exported with an interaction walkthrough to devs for smooth delivery.

Unique selling propositions

Email → Action → Calendar

Auto-summarizes mail, extracts tasks, and places them into calendar slots.

Adaptive day plan

Re-orders priorities in real-time with wellness & weather signals; preserves focus time.

Human-in-the-loop

One-tap accept, tweak, or defer. Clyra learns personal preferences over time.
