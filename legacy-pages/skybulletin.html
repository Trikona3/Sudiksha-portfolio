Sky Bulletin is a sleek, user-centered weather dashboard designed to simplify forecast access for both frequent travelers and long-range planners. It supports rapid comparisons across cities and calendar-based browsing for planning ahead.

The Problem

Weather tools often present dense data without enough structure for fast decisions. Travelers need quick comparisons across locations, while planners need clear long-range views—yet many interfaces make users manually switch contexts, scan cluttered screens, and work harder than necessary to interpret forecasts.

Impact

  • Frequent travelers lose time switching between cities and comparing conditions.
  • Long-range planners struggle to spot trends in a calendar-friendly way.
  • Cluttered layouts reduce clarity when decisions must be made quickly.
  • Interfaces don’t always prioritize legibility and accessibility under changing conditions.

Goal

Design a clear, accessible weather experience that supports both rapid multi-city checks and long-range planning—while keeping information easy to scan and interpret.

The Solution

A clear dashboard built for fast scanning

Sky Bulletin prioritizes real-time conditions, clean calendar-based browsing, and seamless unit conversions so users can move between “now” and “later” without losing context.

  • Intuitive layout that keeps the most important information front and center.
  • Calendar navigation designed for long-range planning.
  • Unit conversions that support quick travel decisions.

A context-aware interface that reflects conditions

Beyond color, the UI adapts to current weather through dynamic themes and subtle motion so the experience stays legible and visually consistent across conditions.

  • Weather-based themes (sunny: vibrant yellow + sky-blue; rainy: deep gray + navy).
  • Animated background gradients (sunbeams, drifting clouds, rainfall).
  • Context-aware typography that increases weight in storms for legibility.
  • “Mood” presets that adjust sounds and micro-animations to match the forecast.
  • Smart location suggestions that recommend new cities when travelling frequently.

Design Decisions

Dual-horizon information structure

Balanced quick “now” checks with calendar-based long-range planning so travelers and planners can use the same product efficiently.

Rapid multi-city comparison

Prioritized location switching and unit conversion to remove friction from frequent cross-city decision-making.

Condition-aware visual context

Added weather-reactive themes and subtle motion to communicate context without sacrificing readability.

Decluttered scan-first layout

Reduced visual noise and emphasized hierarchy so users can interpret forecast data quickly under time pressure.

Target Audience

Frequent Travelers

Business and leisure travelers who switch between multiple time zones and locations each week; they need lightning-fast access to accurate, local forecasts and seamless unit conversions to pack and plan on the go.

Long-Range Planners

Outdoor enthusiasts, farmers, and event organizers who schedule activities weeks or months in advance; they rely on clear, at-a-glance monthly trends and easy calendar navigation to make informed decisions.
