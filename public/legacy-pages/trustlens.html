TrustLens.ai

Case Study

Project Snapshot

Reading clarity for toxic comment sections

A browser extension that adds an interpretive “trust layer” over Reddit comments — surfacing tone and evidence cues without censoring content.

The Problem

Reading online isn’t just reading anymore, it’s decoding. High-frequency readers on Reddit and other forums constantly ask: “Is this thoughtful or angry? Calm or escalating? Even worth my time?” This invisible effort creates a clarity tax, cognitive fatigue from emotional threads, unclear tone, and comments with no visible evidence behind them.

Impact

  • Readers waste time on emotional rants and low-signal threads.
  • Legitimate insights get buried under noise and hostility.
  • Comment tone is easy to misread, especially when skimming.
  • No quick way to see if a comment is backed by sources.

Goal

Reduce the clarity tax by surfacing tone and evidence cues at the moment of reading, without censoring content, so readers can instantly judge which discussions deserve their attention.

Solution

TrustLens in a Nutshell

TrustLens.ai is a browser extension that adds a reading intelligence layer over Reddit comments. It runs locally on the user’s device and reveals tone and evidence badges so readers can instantly see whether a thread is calm, hostile, or backed by sources—without changing or hiding content.

  • Real-time tone signals: green, yellow, and red badges for civility and toxicity.
  • Evidence awareness: quick cues for presence of links and supporting context.
  • Non-intrusive overlay: badges sit beside comments, not on top of them.
  • Interpretive, not moderating: TrustLens never deletes or downranks content.

Target Users

High-Frequency Readers

Reddit and forum power users who read far more than they post and feel drained by emotional comment sections.

Signal-Seeking Professionals

People who skim technical, news, and policy threads but need quick clarity on tone and credibility before engaging.

Future: Creators & Platforms

Writers who want pre-post tone checks and platforms that need clarity signals without heavy moderation workflows.

Research Highlights

We framed TrustLens around the “online clarity crisis”—the mental effort required to decode tone and trust in everyday comment sections—and mapped how readers move from curiosity to exhaustion as threads escalate.

Problem interviews Decks & live demo feedback Trust-signal exploration

Key Insights

clarity tax
  • Readers want to keep content but gain awareness of tone and trust signals.
  • Interpretive cues (tone, evidence) feel safer and more empowering than hard moderation or fact-checking labels.
  • Trust increases when the system is local, private, and explainable.

Design Principles

  • Glanceable first: TL1 color badges must be readable in peripheral vision while scrolling.

  • Progressive disclosure: TL2 tooltips explain the “why” only when the reader hovers.

  • Respectful overlays: badges never cover avatars or voting controls, preserving core Reddit affordances.

Design decisions

Three-layer trust experience

Structured the UX into TL1 (color badges), TL2 (hover tooltips), and future TL3 (detailed breakdown) so readers get fast clarity first, and depth only when they ask for it.

On-device, privacy-first signals

Collaborated with engineers to keep inference local via a lightweight FastAPI + ONNX runtime stack, so tone and evidence analysis never leaves the user’s machine.

Explainable badges

Badge colors are tied to explicit toxicity thresholds and evidence heuristics; tooltips translate model scores into plain-language reasons like “calm tone, no insults” or “hostile tone, no sources.”

Respect Reddit’s visual language

UI is intentionally lightweight and nested near usernames and timestamps so TrustLens feels like part of the conversation, not a separate layer fighting for attention.

Defaults that don’t over-police

Chose conservative toxicity thresholds and neutral copy to avoid labeling nuanced critique as “toxic,” focusing instead on clearly hostile patterns.

Future-ready architecture

L0–L4 architecture separates presentation, transport, inference, and rules so we can evolve models or add new trust signals without rewriting the UX.

Technology

Client

Chrome MV3 extension · JavaScript, HTML

Transport

Local API calls to a FastAPI inference server, with batched requests for performance.

AI Models

Lightweight toxicity model (e.g., unitary/toxic-bert), evidence heuristics, and composite rules engine.

Infra

ONNX Runtime for fast local inference; architecture designed for future multi-modal signals.

Challenges & Learnings

Product direction & pivots

Early ideas shifted from generic AI governance to a focused reader experience. We learned that stabilizing the MVP scope earlier would have unlocked more time for validation with real readers.

AI scope vs. build time

Full multi-model trust scoring was ambitious; narrowing to toxicity + evidence presence helped us ship a coherent experience instead of an unfinished platform.

Missing early-adopter testing

We couldn’t run a proper reader beta before demo day, which meant trust thresholds and copy were assumption-driven. Next iteration will recruit high-frequency readers by week 6 for signal tuning.

Distributed team reality

Part-time contributors across time zones made coordination hard. Over-communication and clearer dependencies became essential to stop rework and keep the build moving.

Unique Selling Propositions

Interpretive, not censoring

TrustLens doesn’t decide what’s true, it helps readers understand tone and supporting context so they can decide what’s worth their attention.

Local & privacy-first

All analysis runs on-device via a local inference server, keeping reading history private while still providing powerful trust signals.

Multi-layer trust UX

TL1 badges, TL2 tooltips, and future TL3 breakdowns create a coherent, explainable system that scales from quick scanning to deep understanding.
