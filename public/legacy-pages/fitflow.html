FLOWFIT

Case Study

Project Snapshot

A women-centric fitness platform

FlowFit is an AI-powered fitness experience built for women across life stages — from period-care routines and senior mobility to self-defense and strength training. The product was designed mobile-first and scaled responsively.

The Problem

Most fitness apps are designed with generic programs and rigid routines that fail to adapt to women’s changing physical needs, schedules, and motivation levels. From period pain to menopause, users are forced to either push through discomfort or abandon routines altogether.

Impact

  • High drop-off due to non-adaptive workout plans
  • Lack of women-specific guidance across life stages
  • Inconsistent motivation and routine fatigue
  • Limited access to safe self-defense training

Goal

Create a mobile-first fitness experience that adapts to women’s bodies, energy levels, and life stages—offering supportive, flexible routines users can realistically sustain.

Solution

FlowFit in a Nutshell

FlowFit is an AI-driven, women-centric fitness platform that dynamically personalizes workouts, recovery routines, and self-defense modules based on each user’s needs and context.

  • Adaptive plans: workouts adjust daily based on energy and goals
  • Life-stage focus: period relief, prenatal, menopause & senior wellness
  • Mobile-first UX: one-hand use, large controls, low cognitive load
  • Hybrid flexibility: seamless home, gym, and on-the-go routines
  • Integrated self-defense: confidence and safety built into fitness

My Role & Process

I led the UX and product design process end-to-end—from research to prototyping— with a strong focus on mobile ergonomics and inclusive wellness design.

  • User interviews across age groups and fitness levels
  • Persona creation and journey mapping
  • Mobile-first wireframes and interactive prototypes in Figma
  • AI concept modeling and system-level UX flows

Design Decisions

Mobile-first, low-friction interaction

Prioritized one-hand use, larger controls, and low cognitive load to support quick check-ins during busy routines.

Adaptive plans over rigid programs

Designed daily workout flexibility around energy, schedule, and life-stage context instead of fixed one-size-fits-all plans.

Quick routine entry points

Introduced short, one-tap routine pathways to reduce drop-off for users with limited time.

Supportive tone and motivation

Framed reminders and prompts to feel encouraging rather than nagging, based on interview feedback about adherence.

Target Audience

Busy Professionals

Working women juggling career and family who need quick HIIT, yoga or self-defense routines that fit tight schedules.

Active Seniors

Older women seeking gentle, bone-strengthening workouts, mobility routines and voice-guided sessions for safe exercise.

Self-Defense Enthusiasts

Women of all ages interested in karate/kickboxing modules for confidence, safety and functional fitness.

User Research

Qualitative research

I conducted in-depth interviews with eight women across different life stages—college students, working professionals, new mothers, and seniors—to understand their fitness habits, motivations, and pain points.

Below are the key questions:

  • What are your primary fitness goals (e.g., weight loss, strength, stress relief)?
  • Which apps or tools do you currently use to track workouts and why?
  • How do you customize workouts to fit your schedule and energy levels?
  • What challenges have you faced sticking to a routine?
  • Would you use self-defense modules, and what features matter most?
  • How do you prefer to receive progress reminders and motivational prompts?
  • What frustrates you about existing fitness apps?
  • What motivates you to return to an app day after day?
  • Which devices do you use most often for exercise guidance?
  • If you could change one thing about your fitness app experience, what would it be?

Interview insights

  • Users want workout plans that adapt day-to-day based on energy and schedule.
  • Complex customization screens lead to drop-off—simplicity is key.
  • Life-stage content (e.g., prenatal, menopause) is almost nonexistent in other apps.
  • Self-defense tutorials are highly valued but often buried under fitness content.
  • Push reminders must feel supportive, not nagging—tone matters.
  • Seamless calendar integration greatly improves adherence.
  • Community features (group challenges, peer support) boost motivation.
  • Quick “one-tap” routines for busy days are a top request.
