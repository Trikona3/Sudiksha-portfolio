Starbucks Mobile App Redesign

Case Study

Industry
Food Service
Role
Research, Redesign
Tools
Figma, ChatGPT
See Full Case Study

This case study focuses on redesigning the Starbucks mobile app’s core ordering and rewards experiences. The goal was to streamline the order‐ahead flow, surface loyalty benefits more prominently, and create a cohesive, user-friendly interface for both new and returning customers.

Through user research, competitive analysis, wireframing, and high-fidelity prototyping, I identified key friction points—like complex customization and buried rewards details—and iterated until the app delivered a faster, more engaging journey.

Challenges

  • Complex order customization flow leading to high drop-off rates.
  • Confusing rewards redemption process, lowering member engagement.
  • Lack of intuitive store locator and pickup scheduling.
  • Poor accessibility for users with visual impairments.

Design Decisions

Simplified customization flow

Reduced friction in drink customization by emphasizing clear, step-by-step option hierarchy.

Rewards visibility in primary flow

Surfaced loyalty and redemption context earlier so users can understand benefits while ordering.

Pickup and location clarity

Made store selection and pickup decisions easier to scan for time-sensitive ordering behavior.

Accessibility-forward UI refinement

Improved readability and interaction clarity for users with visual accessibility needs.

User Personas

Persona Bio & Goals Pain Points
Sarah Mitchell
Office Woman, 32 		Marketing manager who values time efficiency; uses the app to place & pick up her coffee orders quickly without waiting in line. Dislikes waiting or delays; gets frustrated if the app is slow or her routine is disrupted.
Emily Thompson
PhD Student, 23 		Literature PhD student who enjoys the café atmosphere; places orders to earn rewards but loves the in-store interaction and seasonal drinks. Feels ordering via app detracts from her in-store experience; sometimes frustrated by finding good seating during peak hours.
Lisa Carter
Busy Mom on the Go, 35 		Stay-at-home mom who uses drive-through ordering to balance her kids’ needs; values quick, convenient app orders on her way to school drop-offs. Finds it hard to manage orders with restless children; hates long drive-through lines and app errors.

Design Opportunities

Opportunities based on Sarah's journey

  • Improve the notification system for mobile orders to reduce user anxiety.
  • Offer more detailed order tracking, so users know exactly when their drink will be ready.

Opportunities based on Emily's journey

  • Create more seating options or a seating reservation feature within the app to help users secure a spot during busy hours.
  • Provide more personalized recommendations for seasonal items when scanning the app at the counter.

Opportunities based on Lisa's journey

  • Optimize the app's drive-through feature, including estimated wait times for pickup and clearer instructions for using rewards in the drive-through line.
