Project Snapshot

AI-assisted event planning with vibe-first discovery

A concept experience for planning outings faster — surfacing the “vibe” of options and helping users choose a plan with less scrolling and decision fatigue.

The Problem

Event planners regularly receive scattered inspiration (Pinterest boards, Instagram posts, DMs), then spend hours translating that into a cohesive plan. The gap between what clients imagine and what planners can quickly visualize leads to misalignment, last-minute changes, and budget overruns.

Impact

  • 10–25% of events face last-minute changes
  • Changes inflate costs by 15–30%
  • Lost upsell opportunities due to unclear previews

Goal

Bridge the “imagination gap” with fast, authentic visualization—so planners and clients align earlier and make decisions with confidence.

Solution

Vibe Trail in a Nutshell

Vibe Trail transforms unstructured inputs (mood boards, images, vibe words) into polished storyboards and cinematic previews—using authentic venue imagery where possible.

  • Instant previews: see your event before booking
  • Smart suggestions: venues & vendors based on theme & location
  • Centralized hub: visuals, logistics, and collaboration in one place
  • Better alignment: reduce changes and unlock upsells

My Role & Team

I worked as a UX Designer alongside two other designers in a remote collaboration setup. After design sprints and validation, we handed off specs to developers and data scientists and produced an MVP.

  • Design system, stepper flow, and key screens
  • Remote rituals: daily async updates, weekly critique
  • Handoff: annotated Figma, tickets, and API contracts

Key Insights

validated
  • Planners want authentic venue visuals and fast concepts (not just mood boards).
  • AI is most helpful with structured inputs (event type, vibe, time) over free-form prompts.
  • Centralizing visuals + logistics reduces context switching and misalignment.

Market Scan

  • Cvent / Social Tables: robust 2D/3D diagramming, manual setup; enterprise-leaning.

  • Merri: highly photorealistic 3D but longer space-setup time.

  • The Knot: DIY-friendly, light visualization.

Design decisions

Progressive stepper

Mirrors the mental model (Event → Guests → Venue → Vibe) and trims fields per step. Chips + defaults reduce typing, especially on mobile.

AI guidance with human control

Users can regenerate individual shots or swap styles; guardrails stop “AI drift” and keep the brief consistent.

VibeBoard IA

Board → clusters → tags pipeline turns messy inspiration into structured inputs the model understands.

Remote collaboration

Share links, lightweight comments, and vendor brief exports support distributed teams and fast approvals.

Accessibility & motion

Color contrast meets WCAG AA; prefers-reduced-motion disables parallax and uses fade/slide transitions only.

Helpful empty states

Seeded example boards and one-click sample assets help users experience value before committing.

Challenges & Learnings

AI Needs Constraints

Free-form prompts were inconsistent; structured inputs (event type, vibe, time) consistently improved output quality.

Evolving Models

Architecture anticipates model swaps; smaller models can be cheaper without hurting quality for specific tasks.

Feedback Loops

Golden sets + structured evaluation are essential to keep visuals on-brand and usable for planners.

Use AI Where It Matters

Standard functions & APIs remain reliable for workflow; AI reserved for visuals and recommendation boosts.

Unique Selling Propositions

AI-powered storyboards

Turn scattered inspiration into short, shot-based cinematic previews in minutes— perfect for pitching vibe before any heavy production work.

Collaborative VibeBoard

Drag-drop images and links, tag moods, and compare multiple vibe options side-by-side. Designed for remote teams and fast iteration with clients.

Vendor-ready brief

Export a concise, structured spec—shots, constraints, and references—that vendors can quote and execute immediately, reducing back-and-forth.
